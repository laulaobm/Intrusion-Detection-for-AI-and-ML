\section{Conclusion}
In conclusion, it can be said, that the across all three approaches (Random Forest, K-Means, Support Vector Machine), the Random Forest approach achieved the best performance for binary and the multiclass classification task when looking at the f1-score and MCC score.
Additionally, it can be concluded that the chosen machine learning methods are not suitable for multiclass classification task as many, especially minority classes, are missed.
Nevertheless, for the use case of an IDS, the binary classification is used and therefore more important.
Finally, our approach is not comparable to state-of-the-art IDS implementation, which can mainly be attributed to the simplicity chosen for this course and our knowledge.
For future work we could investigates in better optimization strategies gathered across more performative approaches presented in different papers.
Alternatively, a comparison and integration of Deep Learning models could further improve the performance of the IDS, since many studies investigated the advantages of such approaches for IDSs.
